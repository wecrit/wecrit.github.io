Lore of Arkovia

The Astral Sea

          The Astral Sea is one of the four parallel planes, though it is perhaps the least spacially coherent of the parallel planes. Together with the ethereal plane and the prime material, the Astral Sea comprises the conceptual space of the mind in the mind-body-soul triangle of self.

          The Astral Sea mirrors the prime material plane as well as many other planes of existence, as its geography is completely determined by how sapient beings concieve of the world. Real-world landmarks may exist in this plane, but can be diminished or exaggerated depending on how people regard such a landmark. Vast expanses of space between cities may not exist if people don't know the wilds between, and travel by road is usually slower due to this. Many of the creatures which reside in this plane are born from the thoughts and imagination of sapient beings, such as the beholder-kin born of nightmares.

Deities of Elspire

The Eladrin

          The first thing that you notice as you are looking through the lore and history of the Eladrin people, is that they are always referred to as the indigenous people of the land. There are many many references to the "Gifts of the Eladrin", as well as mention of the grand Eladrin city, simply referred to as the "Elf Spire", but most of what you find at first is just too vague to glean concrete details from.

          Eladrin are distinct from their other Elven bretherin, in that they are classically denizens of the Feywild, a plane of positive emotion which lies parallel to the prime material plane. The text somewhat pejoratively describes their features as cartoonish, larger-than-life, and full of passion. This text also seems to refer to them as the "Original Elves" or "Progenitor Elves".

          Much of the information about the Eladrin is fairly obscure, but most of the texts seem to refer to the meeting between the Eladrin and the "First Men" after an event known as the "Exodus". It also describes that roughly 100 years post-Exodus, the Eladrin become opressive lords over the first men, who would later rise up to throw off the throngs of the Eladrin and become an independent state.

          Records indicate that the rule of the Eladrin is in the form of a Kingdom, where a monarch or a small group of holy rulers, usually a chosen Eladrin and their life partners, head the state of the Eladrin people. It is said that the ruler of the Eladrin is annointed as a holy warrior, and is given reverence similar to that of the Elven Gods. Interestingly, it seems that this seat of power has always been located within the Feywild, and that the Eladrin residing in the prime material plane were considered pioneers and wanderers. Another small tidbit you get from this tome is that the hierarchy of the Eladrin is not very strict outside of the monarch, and entire batallions are left to operate independently, commanders simply trusting their subordinates to follow their hearts and do what they believe is right.

The Feywild

          The Feywild is one of the four "parallel" planes, resting along-side,and in conjunction with the prime material plane. It is said that the Feywild is a manifestation of positive emotion, and embraces the genesis of all life. This is in stark contrast with its opposite parallel plane; the Shadowfell, which is said to be negative emotion manifest, and home to legions of undead.

          A very common thread throughout depictions of the Feywild are warnings about the capricious nature of the plane. While it is overflowing with life and positive emotion, these taken to excess bring ruin to those who travel to the plane unprepared. There are many tales here; from becoming eternally lost due to the mischief of pixies and sprites, to spontaneous dance parties where unwilling participants dance until they expire.

The Journal of Hamun Kost

Zvidal, 20 Kovimer, 2612 PEC

This journal is a proclamation. I refuse to listen to the tired wisdom of my mentor. His methods, while reliable, fail to kindle the spark of genius needed to bring the art to the next level. I solemnly swear this day that I will build on "Archmage Champion" Kont's teachings and produce a specimen of such caliber that even his divine strikes fall as but feathers against its flesh.

Today I leave Lucav to find a suitable locale to continue my studies, and to prove to him, and the world, that progress requires sacrifice.

Seldal, 23 Kovimer, 2612 PEC

Today I returned home, the ever-present stinging stench of fish falls upon this sleepy village as it ever has. Such a droll place, but Selia would not let me hear the end of it if I didn't come visit. So little does she understand me, though I know she means well. I am destined for much greater than what this fisherman's town can provide. She insists I stay for Year's Passing, and I suppose it is but two days.

Still, there is much work to do...

Karadal, 5 Kovuns, 2613 PEC

Many days have passed as I search for a reclusive location to practice my art. The King's Road has far too much traffic; traveling merchants and scabby little peasant boys have left no room to study. I think for most, it would be well to kill them and give them some purpose as my guard. Still, I need to avoid any attention.

Tavoldal, 19 Kovuns, 2613 PEC

Nearly two weeks with only one promising location. Too many close calls; however, and I tire of the prying stare of each stranger. One saw me raise a deer as practice, so reports of my art are spreading. Had to pass through the capital, but I think I've thrown off any suspicions. Tensions in Elk's Crossing were far too high, and no-one in Triboar knows to mind their own mending. My stay in Westport has been too long, and I must press forward to find a new berth.

Vardal, 6 Kovanch, 2613 PEC

This is it!

Finally, so few prying eyes. Rumors of trouble in Phandalin have kept the roads here relatively bare, and fortuitously, my supplies are freshly stocked. This small copse is home to a small, abandoned graveyard. It is perfect: out of the way, and plenty of subjects to test on. I do wish they were more fresh; however, the spider only gets the flies which come to it.

So much work to do.

Hesdal, 10 Kovanch, 2613 PEC

Gods am I tired. I have recapitulated Artorious's teachings, re-implemented his practice, and effortlessly produced a small cadre of walking corpses. Faithful servants, though extremely dehydrated and quite weak... but why can I not refine the theory? I need to approach a new angle... I need some sort of necromantic catalyst. I had heard rumors on my way through Westport of a ruined town, Thundertree. A cataclysm befell the boar, and it was touched by the shadowfell. If I had just a bit of material, then perhaps...

Ersdal, 17 Kovanch, 2613 PEC

Took some time, and I am anxious at the thought of some soul discovering my impromptu lab; however, I finally hold in my possession some shadow-touched wood.

It fills me with dread to look upon it, and I feel my hands tense as I grasp it, cold sweat accumulating in my palm. I understand though now, what terrible divine power I need to access.

My path is set, my mettle is steel.

Tavoldal, 3 Benimer, 2613

Damn this wretched journey. Returning to my site and the mindless drones seem to eventually disobey, and two of the bodies are unaccounted for. I am sure I am far enough out that none will stumble upon them, but still, I've been unable to sleep this night.

I am very dull, and of poor mood. Selia is far better for my absence. She would be better yet for my death.

Seldal, 7 Benimer, 2613 PEC

My raving was premature. Just as I promised, I have found a way to infuse the stuff of shadow into my rituals, and the revivified corpse glows with a dark energy. Temperamental at first, but I can still exert my will over it, and the wretch is not only obedient, but stronger than anything Artorious could muster. The method is actually unintuitive, and arose from an error in my calculations, but the results speak for themselves. Sometimes the road to greatness falls upon us, determined by destiny.

I will take some respite, but soon, I will be ready to scale my efforts to several vessels. The only problem is marching them back to Lucav undetected...

Necromancy

Schools

          The school of Necromancy is an often forbidden subject due to the dark nature often associated with it; however, it is important to note that within this school, there are generally three very different types of effects. The first is necrolyzation, which is the process in which life force is forcibly transferred, often used crudely to simply suck life out of a creature. The second is animancy, or the process by which corpses and other non-living vessels are animated, generally as undead. The third, generally un-studied discipline of necromancy is so-called "White Necromancy", which deals in the preservation of the dead and halting decay of the body.

History

          The first thing you find within these texts are warnings against the lure of necromancy. The first tale is about an ancient ruler of the Kingdom of Luftaerga, whose royal mage dabbled in the dark arts. With this dark magic, the King was turned into an Undead creature which fed on the flesh of mortals, but had the mind and countenance of a man. It is said that decades of despair and ruin followed the rule of this King.

          You come accross tales of necromancy used on the contentent and some of the roots of the stigma against necromancy. It seems that necromancy is often associated and conflated with the "original sin", which precipitated in the event known as the Exodus. It says in here that an ancient ruler of a far-off land turned themselves into a Lich and that land was left barren and desolate, with only a handful of survivors fleeing across the ocean to arrive in Arkovia. Due to this, any form of necromancy is strictly forbidden in the new world.

          You find a couple more stories, pouring over these volumes. The first tells of the bloody wars in Arkovia which ravaged the lands towards the start of the second era. You see it references the Hallows as a land "awash with death, the ground soaked in blood, harboring hordes of Dwarf, Ashen, and Goliath Necromancers, raising armies of the dead.". This text seems to villanize these necromancers, and goes into details about their brutal persecution and punishment after man was victorious over their foes. The second story detailed here describes a cult which existed in the later parts of the second era. This cult was said to worship a deity residing within the Shadowfell, whose goal was to punish the wealthy and sinful ruling class, unleashing so-called "Shadowforms" of manifested negative emotion against them. These conjoured Undead were said to have caused widespread death and ruin, as they ravaged the land beyond the control of their summoners. The text never states the name of the deity this cult worshipped, but notes that all worship of this deity is forbidden and punishable by death. It is now believed years after the fact, this faith, and the associated deity, have been effectively eradicated.

          You find more details about one of the earliest stories you discovered on Necromancy. It's the story of Saren Markova I, who was one of the most influential Kings of Luftaerga. Under his rule, much of the land was unified under human banners, and in fact, his rule is said to signify the transition into the current third era of man; however, his Vizier, Alzena Grimleal, delved into dark magic and made contact with a Devil Prince; Kalthax the Binder. Entering into an irrevocable contract, Saren Markova's soul was forfieted to the Prince, and the first of the Vampires was born. The death of the Vampire King would come at the cost of creating the first Markova Tree, and the threat of vampirism still looms over the land to this day.

Orcus

          Orcus is typically depicted as a large, humanoid figure with the face of a goat and blood-red skin. Often he is emblazoned with skulls, and amidst pools of blood. His is the domain of death, and is known as the 'Lord of Undeath'.

          In most depictions, Orcus is shown carrying a sort of club or wand with a human skull resting atop it. It is said that Orcus is a powerful spell-casting entity, able to instantly necrolyze the flesh of mortals.

          Orcus is closely associated with ghouls; undead beings which are born of chaos and evil. These pawns are used in an attempt to establish dominion over both the Abyss and Carceri; however, the infinite nature of the Abyss eludes true rulership, as it is impossible to impose his will everywhere.

Golemancy

          Golemancy is a specific subset of magical study where a semblance of life is granted to nonliving material, generally fashioned into the shape of a vaguely humanoid shape. The axiomatic tennats of Golemancy are: golems are not sapient, and have no free will, and golems cannot harm their masters.

          While there are many paths to creating a golem, all forms of creating a golem involve animancy - a form of necromancy. Usually, some catalytic residue from a living creature is required in the creation of these automatons, whether that is the soul of a sapient being, the essence of a felled elemental, or a caged spirit being. Usually the simplest method of binding such an essence to a golem is using Lyceum Crystals, which have inherent magical energy within them.

          Golems make particularly hardy guardians, but can prove easy to mislead and fool, due to their lack of critical thought. Golems are usually created with hardy materials such as stone or metal; further, due to needing to regulate and contain the entrapped soul energy powering it, often boast impressive resistance to magical energies. If the core of the golem can be reached and placed under an anti-magic effect, a golem can often be rendered dazed or useless.

          Typically, in the creation of a golem, its master forms a telepathic link with it, and is able to issue commands with just a thought. If given an order, golems typically carry it out until new orders are given. If a golem's master dies, it typically carries out its last orders indefinitely, but occasionally, they will go berserk: the the stored spiritual energy slowly taking over the vessel it is in.

Planar Travel

          Most of the first things you find in these tomes are just warnings against trying to perform planar travel. What is useful, however, is that it provides a good overview of the planes, organized according to a cosmological theory known as the "Great Wheel". It talks of the prime material plane and its four parallel planes; the Feywild, the Shadowfell, the Ethereal Plane, and the Astral Sea. It also describes another inner plane known as the Elemental Chaos, which is further subdivided into four subplanes corresponding to each base element; Fire, Air, Water, and Earth. It then describes the outer planes which align with opposing conceptual spaces of lawfulness and morality. Finally, it describes three final planes; a plane of pure positive energy, a plane of pure negative energy, and a plane beyond all of these concepts, which is referred to singly as the City of Sigil. Beyond all of these planes, there exists an infinite continuum of demiplanes which are all nestled within the various spaces between and betwixt the planes, simple referred to as the Veil or the Beyond.

          Actually finding information about travel, you see that the most common way to travel between planes is to discover a planar portal. Portals can be either naturally-occurring or artificially constructed. Naturally-occurring portals exist where the veil between planes is thin. One such area of thinning provided here is relative proximity of the Elemental Plane of Air within the city of Elspire. It goes on to describe methods for discovering planar portals, which generally include divinatory magic and searching for natural leylines of power, which tend to point toward or converge on a planar portal.

          This book talks about creation of temporary portals using high-level spellcasting techniques. It covers a broad range of spells from the Gate spell to the Planeshift spell, but by the descriptions given here, it is both difficult and expensive to gather the components necessary for these spells. It does mention; however, the special significance of the city of Sigil. Sigil is known as a central hub of travel and interplanar commerce where few mortals of the material plane have traveled. Within the city of Sigil lies the Planar Causeway; a temple of sorts with circles of teleportation leading to all other known major planes.

          You find a book which discusses how to create a permanent portal between planes. There are two methods given, the first includes using powers of the divine to tear open a passageway between planes. This method is only ever recorded in legends and as far as anyone knows, no mortal has actually observed this process. The other, more practical method, is to either use planar teleportation magic in the same location for an extended duration (resulting in the creation of a permanent teleportation circle), or by continually wearing away the veil between the two planes. For example, in a location on the material plane thinly veiled from the Feywild, a group of shamans gathering and continually channeling fey-based spells would, over time, wear down the barrier between the planes. Usually these spells are directed toward some object or structure which is to become the planar portal, but this process can be quite long and arduous.

          You find another tome discussing an evidently unproven hypothesis on planar travel which posits that it should be possible to create planar causeways. While the theory itself and symbols are extraordinarily obscure and confounding, given some of the diagrams and supporting text, it seems like the idea is that the infinite space between the planes known as the Veil could be bent into what appears to be a mobius ring shape. THe closing statements state that, were such a causeway to be created, it would actually cause the two planes of existence to coincide, effectively creating not just a portal, but a potentially large swatch of space which exists within both planes simultaneously, and would allow free travel of both inhabitants and energies between the two.

          You begin reading a tome on the topic of planar pilgrimage, and how in recorded legends and myths, entire peoples have moved from one plane to another, citing the example of the egress of the Eladrin from the plane of Arborea. Investigating methods for such mass transport, scholars posit that it may be possible to craft a network of Planar Crest Stones, which can create a sort of planar bubble, almost like a makeshift planar craft which can travel through the Veil from one plane to the next.

          You uncover some obscure studies on the nature of the Veil, but most of this seems like unsubstantiated conjecture and there is a great deal of incomprehensible scribbling and formulas drawn within the margins of this book. What you glean from this is the idea that the Veil itself is extremely dangerous to all forms of life, and that if any creature were stranded within the Veil, it would almost certainly perish. It also states that the Veil is the true realm of the Gods, where their actual beings exist; meanwhile, the manifestations of a Gods Avatar, such as those found on the outer planes, is more akin to the God pushing a hand through the veil to interact with the universe. The thesis statement of the book is that the true form of the Gods is unknowable, and completely alien to mortal minds. This book also posits that throughout the Beyond exists not just the Gods revered by mortals, but beings of untold cosmic strength which constantly assail the Gods, searching for ways to push themselves through the Veil and into our world.

Psionics

          The topic of psionics is highly-debated among wizards, scholars, and practitioners of faith. The mechanics behind the manifestation of these powers are somewhat of a mystery; the only conclusive statement that everyone seems to agree upon is that, unlike magic, psionics do not interact with the underlying magical current of the universe known as the weave.

          While psionics can manifest in extremely varied ways, just as magic, it is unique in that it does not interact with effects of the weave which would hinder magic, such as the effects of a Dispel Magic spell. Interestingly, some psionic abilities require exteme focus, just like spellcasting; however, no psionic abilities seem to require verbal or material components.

          Typically, psionics manifest as the ability to read or alter thoughts, to create semi-solid planes of kinetic force, or to engender a force acting upon an object in the world. What is clear from every source you have is that psionics originates in the mind, and that a consciousness is the key component for manifesting these powers. Psionics are unusable by and on unconscious beings, and cannot be instilled into inanimate objects.
